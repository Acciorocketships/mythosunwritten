Movement archetypes in a gridless world

The trick: don't copy chess geometry — copy what makes chess geometry interesting. Each archetype is a constraint on how a unit relocates. Constraints are what make movement predictable enough to plan around. Which of these excite you? (Select any number — a roster usually mixes several.)

A

Charger (the Rook / Lance)

Moves in a straight line, far, but stops at the first obstacle. Telegraphs its direction; can be body-blocked. Great for "wall it off" play.

B

Leaper (the Knight)

Teleport-hops to any point on a ring at fixed distance, ignoring everything between. Cannot be blocked, jumps walls/units. Unpredictable, slippery.

C

Skirmisher (the King)

Free movement, any direction, but short range. Reliable and flexible — the "infantry." Boring alone, but the glue that holds a formation together.

D

Drifter (momentum / the Glider)

Carries momentum — can't stop on a dime, turns in arcs. You plan its path 2 turns ahead. Wonderful for "lure it into overshooting" traps.

Note the deeper point: movement here is a property attached to a unit type — so a summon-weapon brings a Leaper onto the field, and enemies can be Chargers you must wall off. Tell me which resonate, or pitch your own.
