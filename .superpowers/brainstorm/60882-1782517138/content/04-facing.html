Do units have a facing?

Your spear ("front") and dagger ("diagonal") patterns only have meaning relative to a direction. So the question is whether attack patterns rotate with the unit. This one choice decides whether you get flanking, backstabs, and "turn to guard" — or a cleaner, purer board.

A

Relative facing (recommended — more emergence)

The pattern is locked to where the unit looks. Unlocks: backstab (hit the unguarded rear for bonus), flanking, "spend an action to turn and guard a lane," and ranged attacks that punish exposed facings. Cost: facing is one more thing to manage (turning may cost part of a turn).

B

Absolute patterns (simpler, purer)

Attack shapes are fixed to the board's compass; units have no orientation. Closer to literal chess. No backstab/flank depth, but one fewer system and the board reads instantly. Positioning still matters, just less richly.

I lean A — facing roughly doubles your emergent vocabulary (it's how "defended pieces" and "discovered attacks" get teeth) and it fits the BG/tactics feel. But it's real added complexity. Which appeals?
