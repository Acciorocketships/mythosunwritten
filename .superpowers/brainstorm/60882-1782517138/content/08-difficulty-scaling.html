What does the difficulty gradient actually scale?

Distance from spawn = enemy level. But "level" can express itself as bigger numbers or as harder tactics. Lean toward tactics and the chess stays alive; lean toward numbers and it turns spongy. Which levers should the gradient pull? (Multi-select.)

The difficulty gradient (top-down, spawn at center)
A

Raw stats — HP / DEF / DMG keep modest

A gentle numeric floor so frontier foes aren't one-shot. But over-rely on it and you get bullet sponges that flatten tactics into grind. Necessary seasoning, not the main dish.

B

Enemy composition / piece variety lean on

Further out = nastier combinations (Frog + Ent + Cat covering each other) instead of bigger blobs. Difficulty becomes a tactical puzzle that demands better play, not just better gear.

C

Squad size & number of commanders lean on

More enemy commanders = more board-change between your turns = genuinely harder to plan (ties to your one-minion activation economy). Scales pressure without inflating any single stat.

D

Enemy commander gear budget lean on

Frontier commanders wear higher-budget gear — so they're literally stronger pieces, and that gear is the loot you came for. The difficulty and the reward are the same object. Closed loop.

E

Enemy AI sophistication lean on if feasible

Smarter focus-fire, flanking, terrain use further out. Highest payoff for "feels harder, not just bigger" — but the most work to author well.

F

Battlefield / terrain hazard optional spice

Frontier fights start with the enemy holding the high ground, more chasms, hazards. Cheap way to vary fights and force you to earn position.

My recommended blend: B + C + D as the backbone (composition, numbers, enemy gear), A kept light, E as the long-term aspiration, F as free spice. Select what you want the gradient to lean on.
