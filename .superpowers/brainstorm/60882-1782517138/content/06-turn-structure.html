Turn structure — the skeleton of "multi-step planning"

How many of your units act per turn, and how much can each do? This single choice sets the depth, the pace, and how "chess-like" it feels. Three coherent packages:

1 · Pure chess

You move one unit, then the foe moves one. Deepest calculation, most literal chess. But slow, and an army + a cooldown-juggling hero can feel tedious turn-to-turn.

2 · Squad I-go / you-go (recommended)

Activate all your units (each: move + one attack), then the foe does. Fire Emblem / Wargroove feel. You see the full board and plan a coordinated multi-unit play each turn — forks, screens, combos — at a workable pace.

3 · Initiative + action points

Units act in initiative order; each spends an AP pool across move/attack/abilities (Divinity, BG3). Most fluid and combo-rich per unit, biggest loot-stat surface — but least "boardlike" and the most complex to balance.

My pick: #2. It keeps perfect-information board planning (the source of your forks/pins) while supporting the hero's "reposition → strike → cooldowns" combos and a brisk pace. #1 if you want maximum purity; #3 if you want maximum RPG fluidity. Which fits the feel you're after?
