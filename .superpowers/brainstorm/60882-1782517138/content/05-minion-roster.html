First-draft minion roster (chess → your game)

■ move   ■ capture   ■ both. The twist that makes terrain matter: sliders are blocked by walls/units/gaps; the leaper ignores them. A chasm is a wall to your Rook but a highway to your Knight.

Pawn

Move 1 cardinal, capture 1 diagonal. Cheap, low HP, numerous. Your zone-control chaff — bodies to block lanes and bait trades.

Knight · Leaper

L-hop, move=capture. Jumps over everything — unblockable, crosses gaps/walls. The terrain-ignoring assassin. Hard to wall out.

Rook · Charger

Straight cardinal lines until blocked, move=capture. Blockable → enables discovered attacks & choke-point control. Loves open corridors.

Bishop · Glider

Diagonal lines until blocked, move=capture. Blockable. Covers a different geometry than the Rook — pair them and few squares are safe.

Queen

All 8 lines until blocked. High HP, expensive summon + long cooldown. The capstone — but with the symmetric trade rule, even she dies to two well-placed pawns on a flank.

Two things to react to: (1) do these patterns + terrain niches feel right? (2) Stay chess-pure, or also invent original pieces with asymmetric move/capture like the pawn (e.g. a "Lancer" that drifts slowly but captures in a long telegraphed line)?
