What does "one action" buy?

Agreed turn economy: each round, every player activates ★ their Character + one minion. The remaining question is how much a single activation does — this sets your combo depth.

One player-turn
★ Character acts + 1 minion acts
A

Move or attack (purest)

Each activated unit does exactly one: relocate, or strike from where it stands. Most chess-like (a turn is one deliberate act). Aggression is slow — you must walk into range one turn, hit the next. Maximizes "every step is committal."

B

Move and attack (most dynamic)

Each activated unit may reposition then strike. Enables the "approach → hit," "hit → retreat," kite, and dive plays. Snappier, more action-RPG. Slightly less boardlike since a unit covers more ground per turn.

C

Hybrid: hero moves+acts, minion does a chess move (recommended — fits the two tiers)

The Character gets move and one weapon action (the action-RPG protagonist). A minion gets a single chess move — either step to a move-square or capture on a capture-square (exactly how your Toadstool already works). Heroes feel fluid; minions stay clean, readable, and pure. Internally consistent with everything we've built.

I lean C: it keeps the hero exciting while preserving the crisp, plannable minion board — and it falls straight out of "minions have separate move vs capture patterns." Which feels right?
