The real axis: how legible is the tactical layer?

All three can use your continuous, grid-generated terrain underneath. The difference is what the combat resolves on. Watch what happens to a "fork" (one threat covering two enemies) in each.

1 · Pure continuous

Fluid, cinematic. But threat is a fuzzy cone — the player eyeballs whether a fork lands. Planning becomes approximation. Hardest to make "chess-like."

2 · Mixed (your worry)

Some units snapped to squares, some free. Now every cross-interaction needs special rules. Muddy mental model, muddy code. I'd avoid this.

3 · Soft grid (recommended)

One discrete lattice for all combatants; movement snaps & animates smoothly so the world still looks continuous. Threat = highlighted cells. Forks are exact. Clean to code. Into-the-Breach legibility.

My pick: #3. Your terrain stays continuous and grid-generated; combat borrows a (probably coarser) lattice that every unit obeys. You keep fluid visuals AND get true forks/pins/discovered-attacks. Square vs hex lattice is a fun sub-question for later. Which way are you leaning?
